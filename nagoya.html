Nagoya (名古屋)

Nagoya, the fourth largest city in Japan, was once a key strategic center between Kyoto (the historical capital of the emperor) and Edo (modern day Tokyo) where the ruling samurai government was headquartered for much of Japan's history.

Rather like the nobles of old, my family and I enjoyed a rest in Nagoya on our way from Tokyo to Kyoto!

1. Arrival

After the busyness of Tokyo, and getting floored by culture shock, Nagoya felt like a breath of fresh air. Though still the 4th largest city in Japan, it felt much more manageable and less overwhelming than Tokyo. Multiple members of my family even remarked "wow, I could live here!"

We also enjoyed our stay at the The Lamp Light Books Hotel. As a family of bookworms and coffee lovers the vibes in the lounge felt comforting while navigating so many new things.
